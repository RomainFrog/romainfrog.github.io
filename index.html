About

I'm a PhD student at Meta Superintelligence Labs in Paris, supervised by Thomas Scialom (Meta) and Djamé Seddah (Inria Paris, ALMAnaCH team). My research focuses on building evaluation frameworks for LLM agents — designing realistic, dynamic environments that test how well AI systems can operate autonomously in the real world.

My first publications include ARE, a scalable framework for agent evaluation environments (preprint), and GAIA2, a benchmark built on top of ARE for evaluating agents on dynamic and asynchronous tasks, accepted at ICLR 2026.

I studied Computer Science (ML specialization) at Georgia Tech (2023–2025) and Engineering in Computer Science at Université de Technologie de Compiègne (2019–2024).
